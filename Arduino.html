Arduino


Arduino is microcontroller platform that is easy to use for electronics projects.
It allows you to connect electronics through its pins so you can control things. The Arduino has a microcontroller the has a processor,
kilobyte or two of RAM, erasable programmable read-only memory (EPROM). The input and output pins link the microcontroller to the rest of the electronics.
Inputs can read both digital (is the switch on or off) and analog (what is the voltage at a pin?). Outputs can also be analog or digital.
The Atmel microcontroller on an Arduino board is the 28-pin chip fitted into the socket in the center of the board.
